HOW TO USE TRENDS

Trends are not systems — they're filters. A team going 8-2 ATS in a situation doesn't mean bet them blindly. It means add it as a confirming signal when your other analysis already points the same direction.

The most valuable patterns are ones with large sample sizes (50+ games), clear logical reasons why the edge exists (not random), and that haven't been widely publicized yet. When a trend goes mainstream, books adjust the line to eliminate the edge.

SHARP MONEY

Sharp bettors are professionals who bet for a living. When they move on a game, the line moves. This page shows you what they are doing — in plain English.

📊
Public % vs Sharp %
Public % = percentage of regular bettors on each side.
Sharp % = where the actual money is going (dollars, not tickets).

When these two numbers point in opposite directions, follow the money, not the tickets.
📈
Reverse Line Movement
What it is: 70% of bettors pick Team A, but the line moves toward Team B.

What it means: The 30% on Team B are putting down way more money. Those are professionals. The line tells you the truth.
⚡
Steam Move
What it is: Multiple sportsbooks move the same line within minutes of each other.

What it means: A betting syndicate placed large bets at many books simultaneously. Act fast — the window closes in minutes.
🎯
Closing Line Value
What it is: Did you get a better number than the final line before the game started?

Why it matters: The closing line is the most accurate price. Beating it consistently = you have real edge.
HOW TO READ THE SIGNALS BELOW
SHARP ACTIONSharp money is clearly on this side — line moved against the public
STEAM MOVEMultiple books moved simultaneously — syndicate bet confirmed
REVERSE LINELine moved against public % — strongest sharp signal
WATCHLine is moving — not confirmed yet, worth monitoring
SHARP FADESharps are on the opposite side from the public play
NEUTRALNo clear signal either direction — skip this game
HOW TO READ THE BARS
The gray bar shows public betting tickets. The gold bar shows where actual dollar amounts are going. When gold and gray point different directions — that is your edge.
Example: Sharp money on Team A despite public on Team B
Public (tickets): Team A 28%Team B 72%
Sharp money: Team A 72%28%
→ Bet Team A. Line will move toward them.

THE 3 GOLDEN RULES OF SHARP MONEY
1. Follow the line, not the tickets
80% of bets on one team means nothing if the line moves the other way. Dollar amounts move lines — not ticket counts. Always watch where the line goes.
2. Time your bet around steam
After a steam move, books scramble to adjust. If you can get the number before all books catch up, you are getting closing line value before the window closes.
3. Sharp action means less risk — not no risk
Sharp bettors win 53-58% of the time, not 80%. They win because they bet with positive expected value, not because they are always right. Size accordingly.

THE ACADEMY

Everything the best bettors in the world actually use. No fluff. No systems that sound good but do not work. Just the real edge — explained simply.

📊
Expected Value (EV) — The Only Metric That Matters
If you flip a coin and someone pays you $110 when you win but takes $100 when you lose — that is positive EV. You will not win every flip, but you make money long-term. Every bet works the same way. Ask yourself: am I getting paid fairly for the risk? If yes, the math is on your side.
💼
Bankroll as a Business
Your betting bankroll is startup capital. Never risk more than 2-3% on a single play. A 10-bet losing streak — which happens to every professional — only costs 20-25% if sized correctly. It costs 100% if you are reckless. The goal is to still be in business when the edge kicks back in.
📈
Closing Line Value (CLV)
The closing line is the most accurate price a game will ever have. If you got Thunder -7.5 and it closed -9, you beat the closing line by 1.5 points. Do this consistently and you will be profitable long-term — even through losing stretches. CLV is the professional standard. Win rate is just for marketing.
🛒
Line Shopping = Free Money
Getting +3 at one book vs +2.5 at another on the same game is worth 1-2% ROI annually by itself. Have accounts at DraftKings, FanDuel, BetMGM, and Caesars minimum. The 30 seconds it takes to check 4 apps compounds into thousands of dollars over a year.
🎯
Unit Sizing System
1 unit = 1-2% of bankroll. Free Money plays: 2-3 units. Standard plays: 1 unit. Parlay pieces: 0.5 units. Lottery tickets: 0.25 units max. This is not arbitrary — it is the system that protects capital through variance and maximizes growth when edge is real.
🧠
Fading the Public
Books shade lines toward public bias because public bettors are predictable. They overvalue home teams, primetime games, popular quarterbacks, and recent performance. When the public is 70%+ on one side — and the line is not moving their way — that is your signal. The market knows something the crowd does not.
⚗️
Vig Removal — The True Price of a Game
Every line has vig (juice) built in — the book takes a cut. A -110/-110 line has 4.5% vig. To find the true probability, remove the vig: -110 implies 52.4% — but the real probability is 50%. When you can estimate the true price better than the book, you have edge. Free tools like OddsJam show no-vig lines automatically.
📐
Kelly Criterion — Exact Bet Sizing
The Kelly Criterion calculates the mathematically optimal bet size: (Edge / Odds) = Kelly%. If you have 5% edge at -110 odds, Kelly says bet 5.5% of bankroll. Most pros use half-Kelly (2.75%) to reduce variance. Use the calculator below. Overbetting is the fastest way to go broke even with a real edge.
🔬
Market Efficiency by Sport
NFL sides and totals are among the most efficient markets in sports betting — books dedicate enormous resources. Where edge is most available: NFL props, NBA live betting, early-week college lines, and UFC undercards. The less attention a market gets, the less efficient it is. Go where the book has less data than you.
⏱️
When to Bet — Timing the Market
Bet openers for sharp value on NFL (books open soft, sharps hammer early). Bet game-day for injury news and late-breaking information. Avoid betting right after public news — the line already reflects it. The best number is often available at open or in the 30-minute window right after a steam move when some books lag.
🏗️
Correlated Parlays
Most parlays are negative EV because the book prices each leg independently. The exception: correlated outcomes. If a game goes over the total, the winning team likely scored a lot — their key player going over his points prop is correlated with the over. Books price these wrong. Same-game parlays with correlated legs are the only parlay worth considering.
🗄️
Record Keeping as a Weapon
Most bettors believe they are better than they are. Track every bet: sport, market, line you got, closing line, result, unit size. After 500 bets you will know your actual win rate by market type. Most people discover they crush NFL totals and lose on NBA spreads. That data tells you exactly where to focus.

Never bet to recover
Chasing losses is how a bad day becomes a bad month. Each bet is independent. Set a daily stop-loss (10% of bankroll max). When you hit it — stop. The market will be there tomorrow. Emotional bets have negative EV by definition.
The long game always wins
A professional bettor can go 3-7 in a week and still be up for the year. You need 300+ bets before your win rate is statistically meaningful. Think in quarters. Think in seasons. One bad night means nothing — the math plays out over time.
Betting enhances the game
A well-reasoned bet makes watching sports more engaging — not more stressful. If a bet is causing you anxiety, you are sized too large. Reduce the size until the game is fun again. The goal is to make smarter decisions, not add stress to your life.
Quality beats volume
Forcing 15 bets on a Sunday because games are on is not a strategy. Some weeks the right move is 2 bets. Waiting for genuine edge and skipping marginal plays is a skill most bettors never develop. Discipline is the edge.
Wins feel smaller than losses
Loss aversion is hardwired into human psychology — losses feel twice as painful as equivalent wins feel good. Knowing this does not eliminate it. Counter it by focusing on process (did I make a good EV decision?) not outcome (did I win?).
Responsible gambling tools
Every major sportsbook offers deposit limits, cooling-off periods, and self-exclusion. Use them proactively — not reactively. If gambling stops being fun, call 1-800-GAMBLER or visit ncpgambling.org. There is no shame in using the tools.
🏆
GPP vs Cash — Two Different Games
Cash games (50/50s, head-to-heads): you need a high floor. Build safe, consistent lineups. GPP (tournaments): you need ceiling. Unique plays, leverage against the field, and one or two high-upside swings. Never build the same lineup for both formats.
📉
Ownership Leverage
In a 10,000-entry GPP, if 60% of lineups have the same player and he has a bad game — 6,000 entries bust simultaneously. The 40% who faded him gain an instant advantage. Fading chalk is not just about contrarianism — it is about understanding how field outcomes affect your relative score.
🎰
Ceiling Stacking
Stacking correlated players from the same game (QB + WR, or both offenses in a shootout) maximizes your lineup ceiling. When the game script goes right, your whole stack scores. When it does not, your lineup busts — but so does everyone else who played that stack. The risk is symmetric.
🔢
Expected Points vs Salary
Value = projected points divided by salary (in thousands). A player projected for 30 DK points at $6,000 salary has 5.0x value. In NBA cash games, target 5x+ across your lineup. In GPP, sacrifice some floor plays for 8x+ ceiling plays. Salary efficiency is the foundation of lineup construction.
🧩
The Roster Construction Formula
GPP: 1-2 chalk anchors (necessary exposure) + 2-3 leverage plays (low owned, high ceiling) + 1-2 true contrarians (under 10% ownership, tournament-winning upside). Cash: 4-5 safe floors + 1-2 upside plays. The formula changes by sport but the principle holds.
⚡
Late Swap Strategy
Most DFS platforms allow lineup changes until the player game starts. Monitor injury reports until 30 minutes before tip/kickoff. A single late scratch can turn a 20% owned player into a 60% owned one — or give a backup 3x their normal value. Late information is the sharpest DFS edge available to the public.
